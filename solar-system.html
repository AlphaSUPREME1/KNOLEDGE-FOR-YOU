Introduction

The solar system is a vast collection of celestial bodies centered around the Sun. It includes eight planets, their moons, asteroids, comets, and dwarf planets. The solar system was formed about 4.6 billion years ago and is essential for understanding life on Earth.

The Sun

The Sun is the center of the solar system and contains about 99.8% of its total mass. It is made mainly of hydrogen and helium and produces energy through nuclear fusion.

  • Provides heat and light
  • Essential for life on Earth
  • Strong gravitational force

Inner Planets (Terrestrial Planets)

These planets are rocky and lie close to the Sun.
